# Information Security Policy

## Overview

This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting our organization's information assets, systems, and data from unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, or destruction.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who have access to company information systems, data, or facilities.

## Information Classification

### Public Information

- Marketing materials and public announcements
- Published research and whitepapers
- General company information available on website

### Internal Information

- Employee directories and organizational charts
- Internal procedures and processes
- Non-confidential business communications

### Confidential Information

- Customer data and personal information
- Financial records and business plans
- Proprietary software and technical specifications
- Strategic planning documents

### Restricted Information

- Trade secrets and intellectual property
- Security procedures and access controls
- Legal documents and contracts
- Sensitive personal data

## Access Control

### User Access Management

- Access granted based on principle of least privilege
- Regular review and certification of user access rights
- Immediate revocation of access upon termination or role change
- Multi-factor authentication required for sensitive systems

### Password Requirements

- Minimum 12 characters with complexity requirements
- Unique passwords for each system and account
- Password changes required every 90 days for privileged accounts
- Use of approved password management tools encouraged

### System Access

- All systems must have appropriate access controls
- Administrative access limited to authorized personnel
- Remote access requires VPN and additional authentication
- Guest access prohibited on production systems

## Data Protection

### Data Handling

- Encrypt sensitive data both in transit and at rest
- Secure disposal of data when no longer needed
- Regular backups with tested recovery procedures
- Data loss prevention tools deployed on all endpoints

### Privacy Protection

- Collect only necessary personal information
- Obtain appropriate consent for data processing
- Provide individuals with access to their personal data
- Report data breaches within required timeframes

### Data Retention

- Retain data only as long as business or legal requirements dictate
- Secure deletion of data beyond retention period
- Regular review of data retention schedules
- Documentation of data disposal activities

## Network Security

### Network Architecture

- Segmented networks with appropriate firewalls
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems deployed
- Regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
- Secure configuration of all network devices

### Wireless Security

- Strong encryption (WPA3 or equivalent) for all wireless networks
- Guest networks isolated from corporate systems
- Regular monitoring of wireless access points
- Prohibition of unauthorized wireless devices

### Remote Access

- VPN required for all remote connections
- Endpoint security software mandatory on remote devices
- Regular security updates and patch management
- Monitoring of remote access activities

## Incident Response

### Incident Classification

- **Low**: Minor security events with minimal impact
- **Medium**: Security incidents affecting limited systems or data
- **High**: Major incidents with significant business impact
- **Critical**: Severe incidents threatening business operations

### Response Procedures

- Immediate containment and assessment of security incidents
- Notification of appropriate stakeholders within defined timeframes
- Forensic analysis and evidence preservation when required
- Post-incident review and lessons learned documentation

### Reporting Requirements

- All security incidents must be reported to the Security Team
- External reporting as required by law or regulation
- Regular reporting to executive management and board
- Coordination with law enforcement when appropriate

## Compliance and Monitoring

### Regulatory Compliance

- Adherence to applicable laws and regulations (GDPR, CCPA, SOX, etc.)
- Regular compliance assessments and audits
- Documentation of compliance activities and evidence
- Remediation of identified compliance gaps

### Security Monitoring

- 24/7 monitoring of security events and alerts
- Regular security assessments and vulnerability scans
- Continuous monitoring of user activities and system access
- Automated threat detection and response capabilities

### Training and Awareness

- Annual security awareness training for all employees
- Specialized training for IT and security personnel
- Regular phishing simulation exercises
- Security awareness communications and updates

## Vendor and Third-Party Management

### Due Diligence

- Security assessments of all vendors with system access
- Contractual security requirements and obligations
- Regular review of vendor security practices
- Right to audit vendor security controls

### Data Sharing

- Formal agreements required for all data sharing
- Encryption required for data transmission to third parties
- Monitoring of third-party access to company data
- Regular review of data sharing arrangements

## Policy Enforcement

### Violations

- Security policy violations will be investigated promptly
- Disciplinary action may include training, suspension, or termination
- Criminal violations will be reported to law enforcement
- Civil remedies may be pursued for damages

### Exceptions

- All policy exceptions must be formally documented and approved
- Risk assessment required for all exception requests
- Regular review of approved exceptions
- Automatic expiration of exceptions unless renewed
